Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I water my lawn?
A: As we say in this business, “don’t kill it with kindness”.  Over watering can cause “root rot”.  Watering is necessary, but only if needed.  According to the University of Kentucky Agriculture Dept.  watering one inch per week is usually sufficient.  Everyone’s water pressure is different so take a small container, (one inch deep such as a tuna can), and place with-in sprinkler limits and count the time that it takes to fill a one inch container.  Average time to water one inch is  4 hours.  Watering heavy one time per week encourages a deeper root development versus the lighter daily watering that produces shallow roots and can easily lead to a burned lawn during the hot Summer months.

Q: When should I seed my lawn?
A: Due to our ever changing climate, we only recommend seeding in the Fall in this Zone.   Though new seed may germinate in the Spring after seeding, it is usually burned by the now normal temperatures of the Summer of our zone.  Therefore, please be patient, and save your money.  Wait until Fall and let’s build a thick beautiful lawn that you will be proud of.

Q: Though it looks so bad, can I have a perfect weed free lawn?
A: We have never seen a lawn that we cannot begin to improve.  On some lawns we have to be more aggressive than others, and that is where we need a full evaluation or a plan.  A perfect lawn begins the corporation between the service based company and the client.  A perfect lawn requires watering and proper lawn mowing to achieve the maximum results.  Mowing too short is inviting weeds, and mowing too long is causing too much thatch build up.  So there has to be a happy medium, and that is weekly lawn mowing of 3-3 ½ inches, proper watering, and regular scheduled treatments is necessary. 

Q: Why can’t I just do the treatments myself with products from my local hardware store?
A: Well, no-one is stopping you from working in your own lawn, and actually we want you out there with us.  Although, we know that you will find that the products at your local hardware store can be quite expensive when you actually compare your square footage.  The average square footage in Louisville Metro is 4000 square foot.  In others words, you buy a bag of weed and feed for $55.00, and you only need half of the bag.  The remaining amount of the bag program sits on your shelf for a year to only go bad.  Therefore, how much have you saved?  If you love working in your lawn, let us take care of the basics and you can take care of the rest.

Q: Are you going to try to sell me things I don’t need with constant phone calls?

Q: Do I have to pay in advance for the service?

Q: Do I need a soil sample?
A: The average lawn in this area has the same consistency.  Therefore we fertilize with slow release products that are designed for this area.  If you feel you need to take a personal soil sample then please follow our links to self soil samples through the link at the bottom of our page.

Q: What is growing in my lawn?
A: Well the only way to accurately answer this question is for a property visit.  Bermuda Grass is often confused for Crabgrass.  To be thorough, we would appreciate the opportunity to visit your property and offer you a full evaluation.
